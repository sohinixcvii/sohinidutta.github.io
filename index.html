Research Experience:

Cosmology and Epoch of Reionization

Project Title: Interpretation of multiwavelength observation of the Epoch of Reionization from next generation telescopes
Project Supervisor: Dr. Suman Majumdar, Assistant Professor, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT-I), Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India
Project Duration: August 2021-Present
Project Description: We explored the potential of future multi-wavelength observations in modelling the Epoch of Reionization (EoR) using the redshifted 21cm signal from the EoR and the Line Intensity Mapping (LIM) observations of the redshifted 158 µm emission from CII atoms. We have attempted to constrain the minimum dark matter halo mass for halos that host the early photon sources from the CII power spectrum using an Artificial Neural Networking (ANN) based signal emulator and a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) framework. We have performed these operations on the CII power-spectrum at this time and are working on extending the same treatment to the CIIx21cm cross-power spectrum.

Millisecond Pulsars

Project Title: Searching for nulling in Millisecond Pulsars
Project Supervisor: Dr. Bhaswati Bhattacharyya, Reader, National Centre for Radio Astrophysics (NCRA), Pune, Maharashtra, India
Project Duration: May 2021-August 2021
Project Description: Nulling is a fairly common phenomenon in regular pulsars. Essentially, the predictable, periodic pulses of a pulsar suddenly dips below detectable sensitivity of the telescope for a finite period of time. In millisecond pulsars, however, this phenomenon is extremely rare. My project was focused on searching for instances of this curious occurance in millisecond pulsars using radiofrequency observations obtained by the Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope (GMRT) situated near Pune, Maharashtra, India.
